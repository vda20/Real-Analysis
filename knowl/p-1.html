A large portion of this course will consist in studying real numbers, functions of a real variable, continuity, limits, derivatives and integrals. So what is the difference with the Calculus course? Maybe an example will be useful to explain the difference. Suppose Jasmine has just taken the Calculus course, and she is helping Kevin with this problem: If \(f(x)=x^{10}\text{,}\) find \(f'(x)\text{.}\) Jasmine solves the problem and gets the right answer: using the power rule, she finds that \(f'(x)=10x^9\text{.}\) This is what she learned in the Calculus course.
