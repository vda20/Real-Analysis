Let \({\cal P}(n): n\lt 2^n\text{,}\) and this is statement c. in our discussion of PMI above. Statement a. is \({\cal P}(1): 1\lt 2^1\text{,}\) which is surely true. So if we prove the induction step b., that is: \({\cal P}(n)\Rightarrow {\cal P}(n+1)\text{,}\) then we are done, because PMI tells us that a. and b. imply c.
