Given two integers \(n\) and \(m\text{,}\) the greatest common divisor (often abbrievated by \(\gcd(m,n)\) ) of \(n\) and \(m\) is the largest integer that is a divisor of both. So if \(d=\gcd(m,n)\) then \(d\) divides both \(n\) and \(m\text{,}\) and there is no integer larger than \(d\) that does the same.
