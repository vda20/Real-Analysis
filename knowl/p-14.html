In this course, the natural number, integers and rational numbers will also be considered as already defined  3 . So \(\mathbb N\text{,}\) \(\mathbb Z\) and \(\mathbb Q\) are basic examples of sets. If \(A\) is a set, we will use the notation \(x\in A\) to mean that \(x\) is an element of \(A\text{,}\) or that \(x\) belongs to \(A\text{,}\) and \(x\not \in A\) to mean that \(x\) is not an element of \(A\text{.}\) So for example \(3\in {\mathbb N}\) and \(\frac{1}{2}\not \in {\mathbb Z}\text{.}\)
