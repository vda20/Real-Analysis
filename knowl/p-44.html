If \(n\) and \(m\) are integers, and \(m\neq 0\text{,}\) we say that \(n\) is divisible by \(m\) if \(n=km\) for some integer \(k\text{.}\) In this case we say that \(k\) is the quotient of the division. We also say that \(k\) and \(m\) are factors, or divisors of \(n\text{.}\) Another way of saying that \(n\) is divisible by \(m\) is that \(n/m\) is an integer. Note that according to this definition, 0 is divisble by any \(m\neq 0\) (using \(k=0\) as the quotient).
