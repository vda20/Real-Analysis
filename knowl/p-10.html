So we can do all four operations with the rational numbers. But there is still something missing, and it is not something abstract, but actually quite practical. Suppose we want to build a fence for a field (as in a corn or strawberry field, not the math concept of field of the previous paragraph!) so that it will be a square of area \(A\) square feet. Because the area of a square of side \(x\) is \(A=x^2\text{,}\) this means that we need to solve the equation \(x^2=A\text{,}\) and the answer is \(x=\sqrt{A}\text{.}\)
